## Why landscaping plants deserve their own focus

Residential landscaping is less about commodity yield and more about appearance, maintenance, climate fit, root behavior, growth habit, and long-term property use.

## Main landscaping groups

| Group | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Shade and ornamental trees | Shape structure, cooling, privacy, and visual impact |
| Shrubs and hedges | Define edges, screening, and layered planting |
| Flowers and perennials | Add seasonal color and pollinator value |
| Groundcovers | Reduce bare soil, erosion, and maintenance burden |
| Accent plants | Create focal points and texture variation |

## Useful residential planning questions

1. What climate and soil type does the site have?
2. How much maintenance is realistic?
3. Will roots interfere with plumbing, foundations, or hardscape?
4. Is the goal shade, privacy, curb appeal, pollinator support, or low maintenance?

## Common residential tree and plant considerations

- Fast-growing trees are not always the best long-term trees.
- Flowering plants need bloom timing, pruning, and water planning.
- Foundation plantings should respect mature size, not nursery size.
- Forest-style or layered plantings need spacing and long-term canopy planning.

## Common landscaping priorities

- Low maintenance vs showpiece aesthetics.
- Drought tolerance vs lush appearance.
- Native adaptability vs ornamental variety.
- Root control near structures and utilities.
