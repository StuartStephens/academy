## What are taxes in America?

The United States collects taxes through layered federal, state, and local systems. Taxes fund government functions such as infrastructure, defense, schools, courts, and public health.

## Core topics

| Topic | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Federal taxes | Income, payroll, corporate, and other taxes fund national operations |
| State taxes | States vary widely in income, sales, and property tax systems |
| Local taxes | Counties and cities often rely on property and local sales taxes |
| Tax policy | Tax structures shape incentives, inequality, and public budgets |
| Compliance | Filing, withholding, and reporting obligations affect individuals and businesses |

## Common tax categories

1. Income tax.
2. Payroll tax.
3. Sales tax.
4. Property tax.
5. Corporate and business taxes.
6. Excise and specialized taxes.
