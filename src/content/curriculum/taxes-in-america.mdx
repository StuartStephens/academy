## What are taxes in America?

The United States collects taxes through layered federal, state, and local systems. Taxes fund government functions such as infrastructure, defense, schools, courts, and public health.

## Core topics

| Topic | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Federal taxes | Income, payroll, corporate, and other taxes fund national operations |
| State taxes | States vary widely in income, sales, and property tax systems |
| Local taxes | Counties and cities often rely on property and local sales taxes |
| Tax policy | Tax structures shape incentives, inequality, and public budgets |
| Compliance | Filing, withholding, and reporting obligations affect individuals and businesses |

## Common tax categories

1. Income tax.
2. Payroll tax.
3. Sales tax.
4. Property tax.
5. Corporate and business taxes.
6. Excise and specialized taxes.

## Practical individual and business tax concepts

System-level tax structure matters, but many people also need a working understanding of how taxes affect personal and business decisions.

### What a tax write-off means

A write-off generally means an allowed deduction that reduces taxable income. It does not make the expense free; it changes how tax is calculated.

### Common individual tax planning areas

- Standard vs itemized deduction.
- Retirement contributions.
- Health Savings Account (HSA) eligibility and use.
- Education-related benefits.
- Self-employment expenses where applicable.

### Common business tax planning areas

- Ordinary and necessary business expenses.
- Recordkeeping for receipts, mileage, and invoices.
- Entity structure and payroll treatment.
- Timing of equipment purchases and depreciation rules.

## Compliance risks people often miss

- Missing quarterly estimated payments.
- Mixing personal and business funds.
- Weak substantiation for deductions.
- Ignoring state and local tax obligations.
- Treating tax planning as an afterthought instead of a process.
