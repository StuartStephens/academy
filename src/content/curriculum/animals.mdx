## Why study animals?

Animals are central to ecosystems, agriculture, scientific research, culture, and everyday human life. Studying animals helps explain adaptation, behavior, survival, and biological diversity.

## Core topics

| Topic | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Classification | Helps organize animal diversity into related groups |
| Anatomy and physiology | Explains how bodies support survival and movement |
| Behavior | Shows how animals respond to environment, threat, and social life |
| Habitat and ecology | Connects animals to food webs and ecosystems |
| Human interaction | Covers domestication, conservation, and animal welfare |

## Major groupings to know

1. Mammals
2. Birds
3. Reptiles
4. Amphibians
5. Fish
6. Invertebrates

## Useful study questions

- What does the animal eat?
- How does it move and defend itself?
- What habitat does it depend on?
- How does it reproduce?
- What threats affect its survival?

<Alert tone="info" title="Biology study tip">
Do not study animals only as isolated species. Learn how structure, behavior, and environment connect.
</Alert>
