## Why construction matters

Construction brings together planning, materials, labor, tools, regulation, and sequencing to create buildings and infrastructure. Good construction depends on coordination as much as on individual craft skill.

## Core topics

| Topic | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Site preparation | Ground conditions and layout affect everything that follows |
| Structural systems | Frames, foundations, and load paths determine stability |
| Building envelope | Roofs, walls, windows, and insulation affect durability and efficiency |
| Trade coordination | Plumbing, electrical, HVAC, framing, and finishes must align |
| Safety and code | Construction quality depends on both physical execution and regulatory compliance |

## Typical construction sequence

1. Site prep and foundation work.
2. Structural framing.
3. Envelope and weatherproofing.
4. Mechanical, electrical, and plumbing rough-in.
5. Interior finishes and final inspection.

## Construction materials and property review

Construction quality is strongly affected by material choice. In both building and property evaluation, it helps to separate cosmetic finishes from high-impact structural and envelope systems.

| Category | Typical options | What to evaluate |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Structural framing | Wood, steel, concrete | Strength, cost, moisture behavior |
| Exterior envelope | Brick, stucco, siding, fiber cement | Weather resistance, upkeep |
| Roofing | Asphalt shingles, metal, tile | Lifespan, climate fit, replacement cost |
| Insulation | Fiberglass, cellulose, spray foam | R-value, installation quality |
| Interior surfaces | Drywall, plaster, engineered panels | Repairability and finish durability |

## Material evaluation checklist

1. Climate compatibility (humidity, freeze-thaw, heat).
2. Maintenance burden over 5-20 years.
3. Repair and replacement costs.
4. Code and permitting constraints.
5. Energy and insurance implications.

## Practical property-review lens

When evaluating a building or property, separate cosmetic updates from high-impact systems such as roof, structure, envelope, plumbing, and electrical.
