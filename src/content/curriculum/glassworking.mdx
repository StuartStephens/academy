## What is glassworking?

Glassworking is the shaping, heating, cutting, or finishing of glass for functional or artistic purposes. It includes everything from stained glass and lampworking to kiln forming and industrial fabrication.

## Core topics

| Topic | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Material behavior | Glass responds strongly to heat, cooling rate, and stress |
| Tooling | Proper tools affect safety, precision, and finish quality |
| Thermal control | Annealing and controlled cooling reduce breakage |
| Forming methods | Different methods create very different results |
| Safety | Heat, cuts, fumes, and eye hazards require discipline |

## Common glassworking modes

1. Cold working (cutting, grinding, polishing).
2. Stained glass and lead came/copper foil assembly.
3. Lampworking and torch-based shaping.
4. Kiln forming and fusing.
5. Hot glass and furnace/blown glass traditions.

<Alert tone="warning" title="Safety reminder">
Glassworking can involve sharp edges, high heat, dust, and eye hazards. Proper PPE, ventilation, and process control are essential.
</Alert>
