## Why outdoor skills matter

Outdoor competence is not only about adventure. It is about preparation, safety, judgment, and respect for weather, terrain, wildlife, and limited resources.

## What this section covers

| Topic | Core focus |
| --- | --- |
| Camping | Shelter setup, gear, routines, site selection |
| Survival | Emergency priorities, signaling, water, exposure control |
| Bushcraft | Firecraft, tools, fieldcraft, natural-material problem solving |

## Shared principles

1. Preparation is more important than improvisation.
2. Shelter, water, and exposure management come before comfort.
3. Tools help, but judgment matters more.
4. Leave No Trace and local law still apply.

<Alert tone="warning" title="Safety note">
Do not treat online outdoor information as a substitute for hands-on training in dangerous environments. Practice skills gradually and within your ability.
</Alert>
