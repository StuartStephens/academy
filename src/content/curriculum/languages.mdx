## Why study languages?

Learning languages improves communication, cultural understanding, memory, and pattern recognition. It also opens access to literature, history, law, business, and everyday human connection.

## What this section covers

| Language | Core focus |
| --- | --- |
| English | Grammar, style, usage, and global communication |
| Latin | Classical roots, grammar structure, and historical influence |
| Spanish | Everyday communication, grammar, and global use |
| Japanese | Writing systems, politeness, and sentence structure |
| Chinese | Characters, tones, grammar patterns, and modern use |
| French | Pronunciation, grammar, and international cultural use |

## Shared learning principles

1. Build vocabulary and grammar together.
2. Learn pronunciation early, not as an afterthought.
3. Read, write, listen, and speak in parallel.
4. Use repetition and active recall instead of passive review.
