## Why plumbing matters

Plumbing is part of the basic infrastructure of health, sanitation, and building function. Reliable water delivery and waste removal are fundamental to safe homes, hospitals, schools, and cities.

## Core plumbing topics

| Topic | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Supply lines | Deliver clean water to fixtures and systems |
| Drain, waste, and vent systems | Remove wastewater and protect indoor sanitation |
| Fixtures and fittings | Connect people to water use and drainage |
| Pressure and flow | Affect performance, comfort, and reliability |
| Leak prevention | Poor sealing or installation can cause major structural damage |

## Water supply systems

Clean water enters a building through a main supply line, typically under municipal pressure (40--80 psi in most US systems). From there it splits into cold and hot distribution.

### Cold water distribution

Cold water branches from the main to individual fixtures -- sinks, toilets, hose bibs, appliances. Pipe sizing matters: undersized pipes cause pressure drops when multiple fixtures run simultaneously.

### Hot water

A water heater (tank or tankless) heats supply water and distributes it through a parallel set of pipes. Longer pipe runs mean longer waits for hot water at the fixture. Recirculation systems solve this in larger buildings.

## Drain, waste, and vent (DWV)

The DWV system handles everything leaving a fixture.

- **Drain pipes** carry used water away from sinks, showers, and appliances by gravity.
- **Waste pipes** carry discharge from toilets.
- **Vent pipes** connect drain lines to open air (usually through the roof) to equalize pressure and prevent siphoning of trap seals.

<Alert tone="warning" title="Why venting matters">
Without proper venting, draining water creates a vacuum that can pull water out of fixture traps. Empty traps allow sewer gas into living spaces -- a health and safety hazard.
</Alert>

## Pipe materials

| Material | Common uses | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Copper | Supply lines | Durable, solderable, resists corrosion |
| PEX (cross-linked polyethylene) | Supply lines | Flexible, fast to install, freeze-resistant |
| PVC | Drain and vent lines | Lightweight, inexpensive, glue joints |
| ABS | Drain lines | Similar to PVC, common in some regions |
| Cast iron | Drain stacks in older buildings | Heavy, durable, good sound dampening |
| Galvanized steel | Older supply lines | Prone to internal corrosion over decades |

<Alert tone="info" title="Modern trend">
PEX has largely replaced copper in new residential construction due to lower material cost, easier installation, and better freeze tolerance. Copper remains standard for exposed and commercial work.
</Alert>

## Common repairs and maintenance

1. **Leaky faucets** -- usually caused by worn cartridges, seats, or O-rings. Fix by replacing the worn part, not just tightening.
2. **Running toilets** -- typically a faulty flapper or fill valve. Inexpensive to repair and saves significant water.
3. **Slow drains** -- often caused by hair, grease, or soap buildup. A drain snake or enzymatic cleaner is preferred over chemical drain openers.
4. **Low water pressure** -- check for partially closed valves, clogged aerators, or corroded galvanized pipes.
5. **Water heater maintenance** -- flush sediment annually and check the anode rod every 2--3 years to extend tank life.

## Essential tools

- Pipe wrenches (two, for grip and counter-grip)
- Tubing cutter (copper and PEX)
- Plunger and drain snake
- Teflon tape and pipe compound
- Adjustable wrench and basin wrench
- PEX crimp or clamp tool (for PEX work)

## Practical study approach

1. Learn the difference between supply and DWV systems and how they connect at fixtures.
2. Trace the plumbing in your own building to understand layout.
3. Practice basic repairs: faucet cartridge replacement, toilet fill valve swap, drain clearing.
4. Learn local code requirements -- plumbing codes vary by jurisdiction and govern pipe sizing, venting, and materials.
5. Understand when to call a licensed plumber: gas lines, sewer mains, and permit-required work.
