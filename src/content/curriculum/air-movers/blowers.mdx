## What blowers do

Blowers are airflow devices designed to move air with more directed force and often higher static pressure than many ordinary fan systems.

## Core topics

| Topic | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Static pressure | Helps explain why blowers can push air through restrictive paths |
| Impeller design | Shapes flow, force, and efficiency |
| Housing geometry | Affects output direction and system pressure |
| Motor power | Determines operating range and load behavior |
| Application fit | Leaf blowers, HVAC systems, and industrial blowers have different priorities |

## Typical use cases

1. HVAC air movement.
2. Combustion air supply.
3. Debris clearing and outdoor work.
4. Industrial process airflow.
