## Why study Latin?

Latin is no longer a widespread spoken vernacular, but it remains extremely important for understanding classical texts, legal and scientific vocabulary, and the development of many European languages.

## Core areas

| Topic | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Inflected grammar | Meaning often depends on endings rather than word order |
| Verb systems | Tense, voice, and mood are central to interpretation |
| Cases | Noun endings signal grammatical role |
| Roots and derivatives | Latin vocabulary still shapes modern terminology |
| Historical texts | Latin study opens access to foundational documents |
