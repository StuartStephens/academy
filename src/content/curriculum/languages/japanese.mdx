## Why study Japanese?

Japanese combines a distinct writing system, context-sensitive grammar, and important politeness structures. It rewards consistent study of form, listening, and cultural usage.

## Core areas

| Topic | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Writing systems | Hiragana, katakana, and kanji all matter |
| Sentence structure | Word order and particles shape meaning |
| Politeness levels | Register changes based on social context |
| Vocabulary | Common set phrases are highly useful early on |
| Listening practice | Spoken Japanese relies heavily on rhythm and context |
