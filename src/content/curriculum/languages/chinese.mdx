## Why study Chinese?

Chinese is globally significant in culture, business, politics, and history. Learning it involves both spoken sound systems and written character recognition.

## Core areas

| Topic | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Tones | Tone changes meaning and must be learned early |
| Characters | Reading and writing require repeated exposure and recall |
| Grammar patterns | Chinese grammar is different from heavily inflected languages |
| Vocabulary compounds | Many meanings are built through character combinations |
| Listening and speaking | Spoken fluency depends on tone accuracy and repetition |
