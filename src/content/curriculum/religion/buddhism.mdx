## Core context

Buddhism emerged from the teachings associated with the Buddha and focuses on suffering, impermanence, ethical practice, and paths toward liberation.

## Major themes

| Theme | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Suffering and impermanence | Central to Buddhist understanding of human life |
| Ethical practice | Conduct, intention, and discipline matter deeply |
| Meditation and mind training | Practice is central to transformation |
| Tradition diversity | Theravada, Mahayana, and Vajrayana differ in emphasis and form |

## Important study areas

1. Four Noble Truths and Eightfold Path.
2. Meditation and ethical discipline.
3. Monastic and lay traditions.
4. Regional and historical Buddhist diversity.
