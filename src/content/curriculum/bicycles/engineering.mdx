## Why bicycles are good engineering systems

Bicycles are compact examples of structural design, mechanical advantage, friction, rolling resistance, fatigue, and human power transfer.

## Core engineering topics

| Topic | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Load paths | Frames and wheels must manage rider and terrain forces |
| Materials | Steel, aluminum, carbon, and titanium behave differently |
| Drivetrain efficiency | Power transfer depends on alignment, friction, and gearing |
| Aerodynamics | Air resistance strongly affects higher-speed riding |
| Fatigue and durability | Repeated loading shapes long-term reliability |

## Typical engineering tradeoffs

1. Weight vs durability.
2. Stiffness vs comfort.
3. Aerodynamics vs practicality.
4. Complexity vs serviceability.
