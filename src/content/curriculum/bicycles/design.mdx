## What bicycle design covers

Bicycle design is the relationship between frame geometry, fit, wheel size, components, and intended riding purpose. A bicycle designed for commuting, racing, touring, or trail riding will behave differently because its geometry and parts serve different priorities.

## Core design topics

| Topic | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Frame geometry | Affects handling, stability, and body position |
| Rider fit | Controls comfort, efficiency, and injury risk |
| Wheel and tire choice | Changes rolling behavior, grip, and terrain use |
| Brake and drivetrain layout | Shapes control, maintenance, and performance |
| Intended use | Purpose should guide design decisions |

## Key design questions

- Is the bicycle built for speed, stability, cargo, climbing, or rough terrain?
- How aggressive or upright should the rider position be?
- How much tire volume and clearance are needed?
- What compromises are acceptable for weight, durability, and repairability?
