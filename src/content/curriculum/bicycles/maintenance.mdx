## Why maintenance matters

Bicycles are simple machines, but small wear problems can quickly affect safety and ride quality. Routine maintenance prevents bigger repairs and improves reliability.

## Core maintenance topics

| Topic | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Tire pressure and condition | Directly affects safety, speed, and comfort |
| Brakes | Critical for stopping power and control |
| Chain and drivetrain | Wear affects shifting and efficiency |
| Bolts and bearings | Loose or worn parts can lead to failure |
| Cleaning and lubrication | Reduces wear and improves performance |

## Basic maintenance routine

1. Check tires, brakes, and quick-release/axle security.
2. Clean dirt and inspect wear points.
3. Lubricate chain correctly.
4. Confirm shifting and braking function.
5. Address noise or looseness early.
