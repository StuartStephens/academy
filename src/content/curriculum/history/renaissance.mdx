## Core context

The Renaissance was a period of cultural and intellectual change associated with renewed study of classical sources, urban wealth, artistic innovation, and evolving political life.

## Major themes

| Theme | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Humanism | Scholars emphasized classical learning and human-centered inquiry |
| Artistic innovation | New techniques transformed painting, sculpture, and architecture |
| Urban patronage | Wealthy cities and elites financed cultural production |
| Intellectual transition | The period helped prepare the ground for later scientific and political change |

## Key turning points

1. Growth of Italian city-states and patronage networks.
2. Recovery and reinterpretation of classical texts.
3. Expansion of major artistic and architectural movements.
4. Spread of Renaissance ideas across Europe.
5. Influence on later scientific and political development.

## Long-term impact

The Renaissance shaped education, art, intellectual life, and the cultural foundations of early modern Europe.
