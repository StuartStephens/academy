## Core context

The Space Race was a major arena of Cold War competition. Scientific and technological success became a way for states to demonstrate power, organization, and ideological prestige.

## Major themes

| Theme | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Technological competition | Science became a strategic and political tool |
| National prestige | Space achievements were used to symbolize national strength |
| State investment | Large public programs drove research and engineering advances |
| Cold War rivalry | Space exploration was tied to military and geopolitical competition |

## Key turning points

1. Early Soviet achievements in space.
2. US commitment to lunar landing goals.
3. Rapid advances in aerospace engineering.
4. Apollo 11 moon landing in 1969.
5. Space exploration becomes part of long-term scientific infrastructure.

## Long-term impact

The moon landing symbolized US technological achievement and shaped public imagination, research priorities, and the role of science in national identity.
