## Core context

The Afghanistan War began after 9/11 and evolved from a campaign against al-Qaeda and the Taliban into a long conflict involving counterinsurgency, occupation, and attempted state-building.

## Major themes

| Theme | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Counterterrorism | Initial war aims focused on al-Qaeda and Taliban removal |
| State-building | Intervention expanded into governance and institution-building |
| Insurgency | Long conflict exposed limits of military power |
| Withdrawal and collapse | End-state planning became a central issue |

## Key turning points

1. Initial invasion and Taliban removal from major power centers.
2. Expansion of mission and long occupation.
3. Persistent insurgency and shifting strategy.
4. Negotiation and withdrawal decisions.
5. Taliban return to national control.

## Long-term impact

The Afghanistan War became a major case study in intervention limits, counterinsurgency, alliance strain, and the difficulty of translating military power into durable political order.
