## Core context

The Great Depression was a severe economic crisis marked by unemployment, bank failures, reduced production, and broad social hardship. The New Deal responded with emergency relief, reform, and public investment.

## Major themes

| Theme | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Economic collapse | Industrial and financial systems failed at large scale |
| State intervention | Federal government took a more active role in the economy |
| Labor and welfare | New institutions changed relationships between workers, employers, and the state |
| Political realignment | The crisis reshaped national political coalitions |

## Key turning points

1. Market crash and banking crisis.
2. Widespread unemployment and social instability.
3. Roosevelt administration launches New Deal programs.
4. Federal reforms expand regulatory and welfare roles.
5. Recovery remains incomplete until wartime mobilization.

## Long-term impact

The Great Depression and New Deal changed expectations about the role of government in economic life, public welfare, and regulation.
