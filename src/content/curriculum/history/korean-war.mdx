## Core context

The Korean War began after the division of Korea following World War II and quickly became one of the first major armed conflicts of the Cold War.

## Major themes

| Theme | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Postwar division | The peninsula was split into rival political systems |
| International intervention | The war drew in the US, UN forces, China, and Soviet support structures |
| Limited war | Conflict remained intense without becoming direct global war between superpowers |
| Armistice without peace treaty | The war ended in stalemate rather than full settlement |

## Key turning points

1. North Korean invasion of the South.
2. UN and US intervention.
3. Chinese intervention changes the war's trajectory.
4. Front stabilizes near the 38th parallel.
5. Armistice agreement in 1953.

## Long-term impact

The Korean War hardened Cold War divisions, militarized the peninsula, and left a political conflict that still shapes East Asian geopolitics.
