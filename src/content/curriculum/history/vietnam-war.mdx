## Core context

The Vietnam War emerged from anti-colonial struggle, Cold War containment policy, and internal political conflict in Vietnam. It became one of the most controversial wars in modern US history.

## Major themes

| Theme | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Containment policy | US intervention was shaped by Cold War strategic thinking |
| Guerrilla and insurgent war | Conventional military power faced political and tactical limits |
| Public opinion | Media and protest deeply affected domestic legitimacy |
| State legitimacy | Political stability mattered as much as battlefield outcomes |

## Key turning points

1. Escalation of US involvement.
2. Large-scale military intervention and bombing campaigns.
3. Tet Offensive shifts political perception.
4. Vietnamization and drawdown efforts.
5. Final North Vietnamese victory and reunification.

## Long-term impact

The Vietnam War reshaped US foreign policy debates, military doctrine, public trust in government, and how policymakers think about intervention and counterinsurgency.
