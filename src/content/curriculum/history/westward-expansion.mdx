## Core context

Westward expansion involved territorial growth, migration, infrastructure development, and violent displacement of Indigenous peoples. It was central to the growth of the United States and to major conflicts over land, power, and slavery.

## Major themes

| Theme | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Territorial growth | Expansion reshaped national scale and political ambition |
| Indigenous dispossession | Native nations faced removal, warfare, and broken treaties |
| Slavery expansion | New territories intensified sectional conflict |
| Infrastructure and settlement | Railroads, land policy, and migration changed the economy |

## Key turning points

1. Louisiana Purchase and early expansion.
2. Removal policies and frontier violence.
3. War and treaty-driven territorial gains.
4. Settlement, railroad growth, and federal land policy.
5. Intensified sectional conflict over expansion and slavery.

## Long-term impact

Westward expansion shaped national identity and economic development while entrenching dispossession, violence, and major legal and political conflicts.
