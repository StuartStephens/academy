## Core context

The French Revolution emerged from fiscal crisis, social inequality, and political breakdown in the old regime. It reshaped ideas of citizenship, sovereignty, and political legitimacy.

## Major themes

| Theme | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Popular sovereignty | Political authority shifted away from monarchy |
| Social hierarchy | Traditional privilege came under attack |
| Revolutionary violence | Political transformation became tied to coercion and fear |
| Nationalism | The revolution influenced modern political identity and mobilization |

## Key turning points

1. Fiscal crisis and collapse of royal authority.
2. National Assembly and revolutionary reform.
3. Radicalization and internal conflict.
4. Reign of Terror and revolutionary violence.
5. Rise of Napoleon and transformation of Europe.

## Long-term impact

The French Revolution shaped modern political language around rights, citizenship, nationalism, and mass politics far beyond France.
