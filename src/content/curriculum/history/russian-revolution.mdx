## Core context

The Russian Revolution grew from war pressure, economic hardship, autocratic weakness, and revolutionary organizing. It led to the fall of the tsarist regime and the rise of the Soviet state.

## Major themes

| Theme | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| State collapse | Imperial authority broke under war and internal crisis |
| Revolutionary ideology | Marxist politics shaped leadership and state formation |
| Civil war | Violent struggle determined post-revolutionary power |
| One-party state | Political centralization redefined governance and repression |

## Key turning points

1. Crisis of the tsarist regime during World War I.
2. February Revolution and fall of the monarchy.
3. October Revolution and Bolshevik seizure of power.
4. Civil War and consolidation of authority.
5. Formation of the Soviet Union.

## Long-term impact

The Russian Revolution shaped twentieth-century ideology, global communist movements, and the geopolitical foundations of the Cold War.
