## Core context

The Iraq War began in 2003 under the justification of security threats and regime change. The removal of Saddam Hussein led to occupation, insurgency, political fragmentation, and long-term regional instability.

## Major themes

| Theme | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Regime change | Military victory did not guarantee political stability |
| Occupation policy | Post-invasion decisions deeply shaped later conflict |
| Sectarian conflict | Internal fragmentation intensified instability |
| Regional effects | The war altered power balances across the Middle East |

## Key turning points

1. Invasion and rapid collapse of existing regime.
2. Occupation and restructuring of state institutions.
3. Insurgency and sectarian violence intensify.
4. Strategic adjustment and surge period.
5. Long aftermath and regional consequences.

## Long-term impact

The Iraq War reshaped US credibility debates, regional politics, intervention doctrine, and public trust in official war justifications.
