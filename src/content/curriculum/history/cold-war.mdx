## Core context

The Cold War was not a single battlefield war but a global political, ideological, military, and technological rivalry between the United States and the Soviet Union.

## Major themes

| Theme | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Ideological rivalry | Capitalism and communism competed for influence |
| Nuclear deterrence | Mutual destruction shaped strategic behavior |
| Proxy conflict | Competition played out through regional wars |
| Intelligence and influence | Espionage, propaganda, and alliance systems mattered deeply |

## Key turning points

1. Breakdown of wartime alliance after World War II.
2. Formation of competing military and political blocs.
3. Nuclear arms race and deterrence doctrine.
4. Regional proxy wars and crises.
5. Soviet decline and end of the Cold War order.

## Long-term impact

The Cold War shaped modern military institutions, alliance politics, intelligence systems, and the political development of many regions across the world.
