## Core context

The American Revolution grew from disputes over taxation, representation, imperial control, and colonial self-government. Tensions escalated into war and eventually into formal independence.

## Major themes

| Theme | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Taxation and representation | Colonists challenged parliamentary control without representation |
| Sovereignty | Colonies moved from protest to independence |
| Alliance building | French support changed the military balance |
| Founding ideas | Rights, liberty, and republican government became central political language |

## Key turning points

1. Escalation after British taxation and enforcement policies.
2. Colonial resistance and the move from protest to armed conflict.
3. Declaration of Independence in 1776.
4. French alliance and decisive military support.
5. British defeat at Yorktown and treaty settlement.

## Long-term impact

The Revolution created an independent United States, but it also left unresolved questions about slavery, citizenship, federal power, and participation in public life.
