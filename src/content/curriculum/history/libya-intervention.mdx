## Core context

The 2011 Libya intervention occurred during the Arab uprisings and was justified in part through civilian protection and international action. The fall of Muammar Gaddafi was followed by fragmentation and prolonged instability.

## Major themes

| Theme | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Humanitarian intervention | External force was framed around civilian protection |
| Regime collapse | Removing leadership did not create stable governance |
| Fragmentation | Militias and competing factions undermined central control |
| International limits | External intervention had weak post-conflict follow-through |

## Key turning points

1. Uprising during the Arab Spring period.
2. International authorization and military action.
3. Collapse of existing regime.
4. Weak transition and growing factional conflict.
5. Prolonged instability and foreign influence.

## Long-term impact

Libya became a major example in debates over humanitarian intervention, regime change, and the dangers of weak postwar planning.
