## Core context

The attacks of September 11, 2001 transformed US security policy, foreign intervention, public discourse, and the legal framework of surveillance and emergency power.

## Major themes

| Theme | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Terrorism and response | Non-state violence triggered major military and legal changes |
| Security state expansion | Surveillance, detention, and homeland security powers grew |
| Global intervention | The US launched long conflicts with broad geopolitical effects |
| Public memory | 9/11 became a defining event in modern US political culture |

## Key turning points

1. The September 11 attacks.
2. US invasion of Afghanistan.
3. Security and counterterror policy expansion.
4. Broadening of conflict and strategic mission creep.
5. Long-term reassessment of intervention and national security policy.

## Long-term impact

9/11 and the War on Terror reshaped global intervention, alliance politics, civil liberties debates, and the structure of modern US security institutions.
