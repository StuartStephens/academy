## Core context

The Civil War emerged from deep conflicts over slavery, political power, states' rights, and the future direction of the United States. Secession transformed political crisis into war.

## Major themes

| Theme | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Slavery | Central cause of sectional conflict and secession |
| Union vs Confederacy | Competing visions of national sovereignty |
| Industrial and logistical power | Material capacity shaped military outcomes |
| Reconstruction legacy | The war ended slavery but not racial inequality |

## Key turning points

1. Secession after Lincoln's election.
2. Outbreak of war at Fort Sumter.
3. Emancipation Proclamation shifts war aims.
4. Major Union victories weaken Confederate capacity.
5. Confederate surrender and transition to Reconstruction.

## Long-term impact

The Civil War preserved the Union and ended legal slavery, but the postwar settlement left major struggles over civil rights, federal power, and memory unresolved.
