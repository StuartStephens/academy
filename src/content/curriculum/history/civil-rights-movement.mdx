## Core context

The Civil Rights Movement challenged segregation, voter suppression, and racial discrimination through activism, legal action, public protest, and federal intervention.

## Major themes

| Theme | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Direct action | Boycotts, marches, and sit-ins changed public attention and pressure |
| Federal law | National legislation became central to civil rights enforcement |
| Voting rights | Political participation was a core battleground |
| Movement diversity | Multiple organizations and strategies shaped the movement |

## Key turning points

1. Legal challenges to segregation.
2. Mass protest and local organizing campaigns.
3. National media attention to violence and repression.
4. Civil Rights Act and Voting Rights Act.
5. Continued struggle over equality after major legislation.

## Long-term impact

The Civil Rights Movement transformed US law and political culture, but many struggles over race, equality, and state power continued after its major legislative victories.
