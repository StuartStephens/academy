## What is metalworking?

Metalworking is the shaping, cutting, joining, finishing, or heat-treating of metal to produce tools, parts, structures, or decorative work. It sits at the intersection of craft, manufacturing, and engineering.

## Core topics

| Topic | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Material selection | Different metals behave differently under load and heat |
| Cutting and shaping | Sawing, grinding, machining, and forming all affect final accuracy |
| Joining | Welding, brazing, fastening, and mechanical joints serve different purposes |
| Heat treatment | Temperature changes strength, hardness, and ductility |
| Finishing | Surface preparation affects corrosion resistance and appearance |

## Common metalworking areas

1. Fabrication and layout.
2. Machining and tolerance work.
3. Welding and joining.
4. Forging and forming.
5. Finishing and corrosion protection.

<Alert tone="warning" title="Safety reminder">
Metalworking can involve heat, sparks, sharp edges, noise, dust, and heavy tools. Eye protection, ventilation, gloves where appropriate, and proper machine discipline matter.
</Alert>
