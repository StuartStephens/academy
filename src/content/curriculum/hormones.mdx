## What are hormones?

Hormones are chemical messengers that travel through the body and help regulate processes like metabolism, growth, stress response, sleep, mood, and reproduction. They are central to endocrine system function.

## Core topics

| Topic | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Endocrine glands | Different glands produce different signaling molecules |
| Hormone signaling | Hormones influence target tissues through receptors |
| Feedback loops | Many hormones are regulated through negative feedback |
| Growth and metabolism | Hormones shape energy use, tissue development, and repair |
| Reproduction and stress | Hormones affect fertility, adaptation, and survival response |

## Major glands and systems

1. Pituitary gland
2. Thyroid gland
3. Adrenal glands
4. Pancreas
5. Ovaries and testes

## Important hormone examples

- Insulin
- Cortisol
- Thyroid hormones
- Estrogen
- Testosterone
- Growth hormone

<Alert tone="info" title="Biology principle">
Hormones rarely act alone. Most endocrine effects come from interacting systems, feedback loops, and receptor sensitivity.
</Alert>
