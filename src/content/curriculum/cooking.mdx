## Why cooking matters

Cooking is applied chemistry, timing, and sensory judgment. Good cooking improves safety, texture, flavor, and consistency while reducing waste and guesswork.

## Shared kitchen principles

1. Ingredient quality matters before technique starts.
2. Heat control is usually more important than extreme complexity.
3. Timing and rest periods affect texture as much as seasoning.
4. Clean handling and food safety matter as much as flavor.
5. A sharp knife and a hot pan solve most problems.
