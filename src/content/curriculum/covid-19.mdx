## What was COVID-19?

COVID-19 was a global pandemic caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. It affected public health systems, economies, travel, education, work, and political decision-making across the world.

## Core topics

| Topic | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Viral transmission | Explains how outbreaks spread through populations |
| Public health response | Policy and healthcare systems shaped outcomes |
| Vaccination and immunity | Central to disease mitigation and severity reduction |
| Social impact | The pandemic changed work, school, mobility, and trust |
| Long-term lessons | Preparedness and communication remain major issues |

## Major phases to understand

1. Early outbreak recognition and uncertainty.
2. Rapid global spread and emergency response.
3. Public health restrictions and healthcare strain.
4. Vaccine development and rollout.
5. Ongoing adaptation, variants, and recovery phases.

<Alert tone="info" title="Study approach">
COVID-19 is best studied through biology, epidemiology, public policy, communication, and social behavior together rather than as a purely medical story.
</Alert>
