## Why chemical safety matters

Many cleaning problems come from using the wrong product, too much product, poor ventilation, or dangerous chemical combinations rather than from not using enough strength.

## Practical rules

1. Read the label first.
2. Use the mildest effective product.
3. Ventilate the area.
4. Keep products away from children, pets, and food-prep mistakes.
5. Never mix unknown chemicals.

## About disinfecting wipes

Disinfecting wipes can be useful, but they are not harmless just because they are convenient.

### Common issues

- Skin and eye irritation.
- Breathing irritation in poorly ventilated areas.
- Surface damage on delicate coatings, electronics, unfinished wood, or certain finishes.
- Misuse on food-contact areas without following label guidance.

<Alert tone="warning" title="Simple principle">
Stronger chemistry is not automatically better cleaning. Match the product to the surface and the actual cleaning goal.
</Alert>
