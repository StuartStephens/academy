## Bathroom cleaning priorities

Bathrooms combine soap residue, mineral buildup, body oils, moisture, and microorganisms. Good bathroom cleaning is about routine, ventilation, and using the least aggressive product that fits the job.

## Cleaning a toilet properly

### Safer process

1. Flush first if needed.
2. Apply toilet-bowl cleaner inside the bowl and let it dwell according to product guidance.
3. Use a toilet brush to scrub under the rim and water line.
4. Wipe the seat, lid, tank handle, and outer surfaces with a separate cloth or disposable material.
5. Rinse or replace cleaning tools afterward and wash hands.

### Avoid

- Using the same cloth on the toilet and other bathroom surfaces.
- Splashing stronger chemicals around unnecessarily.
- Mixing bowl cleaners with bleach or other agents.

## Cleaning a sink properly

### Safer process

1. Remove debris and hair first.
2. Use a non-abrasive cleaner matched to the sink surface.
3. Clean faucet handles, the drain area, and splash zones.
4. Rinse and dry to reduce spotting and mineral buildup.

### Avoid

- Abrasives on polished finishes unless the surface clearly tolerates them.
- Letting standing cleaner sit too long on plated or delicate fixtures.

## Cleaning a shower properly

Shower cleaning is often about soap scum, mineral deposits, mildew risk, and poor ventilation.

### Safer process

1. Improve ventilation first.
2. Rinse surfaces to soften residue.
3. Use a bathroom cleaner suited to tile, glass, acrylic, or stone.
4. Scrub gently with non-scratching tools.
5. Rinse thoroughly.
6. Dry or squeegee where practical to reduce buildup.

### Avoid

- Using harsh abrasives on acrylic or coated surfaces.
- Using acidic products on stone unless specifically compatible.
- Letting moisture sit long-term in poorly ventilated spaces.

## How to clean plumbing areas properly

For visible plumbing areas, the goal is usually to clean surfaces, reduce buildup, and notice early leaks or corrosion.

### Good practice

- Wipe exposed pipes and shutoff areas dry rather than soaking them.
- Check for leaks, corrosion, or mineral crust while cleaning.
- Clean around traps and under-sink cabinets so drips are easier to notice.
- Use mild cleaners first before stronger products.

## What about drains?

Drain maintenance should focus on prevention first.

### Safer drain habits

1. Remove hair and debris manually where possible.
2. Flush periodically with hot water if the plumbing/surface allows.
3. Use strainers to reduce buildup.
4. Escalate carefully if slow drainage continues.

### Important caution

Repeated harsh chemical drain cleaners can damage some plumbing components, create fume hazards, and make future work more dangerous.

<Alert tone="warning" title="Plumbing chemical caution">
Do not mix drain products, bleach, acids, or other strong cleaners. If you are unsure what is already in the drain, stop and avoid adding another chemical.
</Alert>
