## Core principle

Electronics should be cleaned with minimal liquid, soft tools, and patience. The goal is to remove oils, dust, and debris without forcing moisture into seams, switches, ports, or adhesives.

## Safe process

1. Power the device off and disconnect it.
2. Remove loose dust with a dry microfiber cloth or soft brush.
3. Use a lightly damp cloth, not a wet one.
4. Clean seams and creases gently with dry swabs or a soft brush.
5. Let the device dry fully before powering it on again.

## Example: MX Master 3 mouse

- Wipe the shell with a lightly damp microfiber cloth.
- Use dry swabs around the wheel and side buttons.
- Keep fluid out of the wheel assembly and charging port.
- Avoid harsh solvents on rubberized areas.

## Avoid

- Spraying cleaner directly onto electronics.
- Saturating cloths.
- Abrasive pads or rough paper products.
- Heavy disinfectant use on delicate coatings unless manufacturer guidance supports it.
