## Why metals matter

Metals are central to structures, tools, machines, wiring, transport, and manufacturing. Their value comes from combinations of strength, workability, conductivity, and durability.

## Core topics

| Topic | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Strength and ductility | Metals can be both load-bearing and formable |
| Corrosion behavior | Environment strongly affects longevity |
| Conductivity | Electrical and thermal performance vary by alloy |
| Processing | Casting, forging, machining, and welding shape outcomes |
| Alloying | Small composition changes can significantly alter behavior |
