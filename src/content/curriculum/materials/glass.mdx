## Why glass matters as a material

Glass is widely used because of its transparency, chemical resistance, and shaping potential, but it is also brittle and sensitive to thermal and mechanical stress.

## Core topics

| Topic | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Brittleness | Glass fails differently from ductile materials |
| Thermal behavior | Heat and cooling rate affect stress and fracture risk |
| Optical properties | Transparency and surface finish matter in many uses |
| Surface and coating behavior | Treatments change strength, cleaning, and performance |
| Application fit | Window glass, containers, optics, and technical glass differ widely |
