## What is bronze?

Bronze is a copper-based alloy, traditionally associated with tin but also produced with other alloying elements depending on the application. It has been important in tools, sculpture, bearings, marine fittings, and historical metallurgy.

## Core topics

| Topic | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Alloy composition | Small changes affect hardness, castability, and corrosion resistance |
| Casting behavior | Bronze is widely used in cast forms |
| Mechanical properties | Useful balance of strength, wear resistance, and machinability |
| Corrosion behavior | Important for marine and decorative applications |
| Historical significance | Bronze transformed toolmaking and early state development |

## Common uses

- Bearings and bushings
- Sculpture and art casting
- Marine components
- Historical tools and weapons
