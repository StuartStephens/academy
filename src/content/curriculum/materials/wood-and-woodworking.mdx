## Why wood matters

Wood is one of the most important structural and craft materials in human history. It is renewable, workable, strong for its weight, and highly sensitive to grain direction and moisture.

## Core topics

| Topic | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Grain direction | Affects strength, cutting behavior, and finish |
| Moisture movement | Expansion and contraction shape stability |
| Joinery | Good joints determine durability and craftsmanship |
| Tooling | Hand tools and power tools change precision and workflow |
| Species differences | Hardness, density, and appearance vary widely |
