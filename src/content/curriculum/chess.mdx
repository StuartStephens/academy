## Why study chess?

Chess develops pattern recognition, calculation, planning, and disciplined decision-making. It rewards both tactical alertness and long-term positional understanding.

## Core topics

| Topic | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Rules and movement | Every later idea depends on clean fundamentals |
| Opening principles | Early development affects safety and initiative |
| Tactics | Short combinations win material or force outcomes |
| Strategy | Long-term planning improves position and coordination |
| Endgames | Simplified positions often decide the result |

## Beginner fundamentals

1. Control the center.
2. Develop pieces efficiently.
3. Castle for king safety.
4. Avoid hanging pieces.
5. Look for checks, captures, and threats every move.

## Core improvement habits

- Solve tactics regularly.
- Review your own games.
- Learn basic mates and king-and-pawn endings.
- Study why moves are played, not just which moves are played.

<Alert tone="info" title="Improvement principle">
Most beginners improve faster by reducing blunders and learning simple plans than by memorizing long opening lines.
</Alert>
