## Why study plants?

Plants are foundational to life systems through oxygen production, carbon cycling, food webs, medicine, agriculture, and habitat formation. They are not passive background organisms; they are active biological systems central to ecosystems.

## Core topics

| Topic | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Plant anatomy | Roots, stems, leaves, flowers, and vascular systems drive survival |
| Photosynthesis | Explains energy conversion and plant growth |
| Reproduction | Seeds, spores, flowers, and pollination shape plant continuity |
| Ecology | Plants interact with soil, climate, animals, and microbes |
| Human use | Plants support food, medicine, materials, and land management |

## Main plant categories to know

1. Flowering plants
2. Conifers and other gymnosperms
3. Ferns
4. Mosses and related nonvascular plants

## Useful study questions

- How does the plant get light, water, and nutrients?
- How does it reproduce?
- What environment supports it best?
- What organisms depend on it?
- What role does it play in human systems?

<Alert tone="info" title="Botany study tip">
Learn plants as living systems linked to soil, climate, pollinators, and human use, not just as names to memorize.
</Alert>
