## Why study materials?

Materials determine how objects perform, fail, age, and can be manufactured. Choosing the right material affects strength, flexibility, weight, corrosion resistance, insulation, cost, and repairability.

## What this section covers

| Topic | Core focus |
| --- | --- |
| Plastics | Polymers, shaping methods, flexibility, durability |
| Paper | Fiber structure, production, coating, and use |
| Wood and woodworking | Grain, joinery, moisture, shaping, and craft |
| Concrete | Aggregates, cement chemistry, curing, structural use |
| Bronze | Alloy behavior, casting, durability, and historical use |

## Shared material questions

1. What loads or stresses must the material survive?
2. How does it behave with heat, water, pressure, or time?
3. How is it formed, cut, joined, or finished?
4. What are the cost and maintenance implications?
