## Scope of aircraft maintenance

Aircraft maintenance focuses on inspection, preventive maintenance, repair, and documentation under strict regulatory standards.

Compared to automotive work, aviation has much tighter procedural and compliance requirements.

## Core areas

| Area | Beginner focus |
| --- | --- |
| Airframe | Structures, fasteners, corrosion, control surfaces |
| Powerplant | Engine systems, lubrication, ignition, propeller basics |
| Avionics interface | Basic wiring discipline and inspection principles |
| Documentation | Logbooks, traceability, maintenance records |
| Human factors | Checklists, communication, error prevention |

<Alert tone="warning" title="Regulatory note">
Licensing, maintenance authority, and sign-off requirements vary by country. Always follow your local aviation authority framework.
</Alert>

## Typical pathway

1. Enter an approved maintenance training program.
2. Complete supervised practical hours.
3. Pass required written/practical assessments.
4. Work under qualified supervision and build experience.

## Professional habits

- Never shortcut checklists.
- Use controlled tooling and calibrated equipment.
- Escalate uncertainty immediately.
- Keep records complete and legible.
