## What is vaping?

Vaping refers to the use of electronic devices that heat liquid into an inhaled aerosol. These devices vary widely in design, power, refill system, and intended use.

## Core topics

| Topic | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Device components | Batteries, coils, tanks, pods, and airflow shape function |
| Nicotine delivery | Concentration and device design affect exposure |
| Health considerations | Inhalation risks and dependency issues matter |
| Regulation | Products are affected by public health and age-restriction laws |

## How vaping devices work

All vaping devices share the same basic mechanism: a battery powers a heating element (coil) that vaporizes a liquid (e-liquid or juice), producing an aerosol the user inhales.

### Basic device structure

1. **Battery** -- provides electrical power. Can be built-in (disposables, pods) or replaceable (mods). Lithium-ion cells are standard.
2. **Coil / atomizer** -- a wire (often kanthal, stainless steel, or nichrome) wrapped around a wicking material. Electrical resistance generates heat.
3. **Wick** -- usually organic cotton. Absorbs e-liquid and delivers it to the coil surface by capillary action.
4. **Tank / pod / cartridge** -- holds the e-liquid reservoir. Capacity ranges from ~1 mL (pods) to 5+ mL (tanks).
5. **Airflow** -- controls draw resistance. Tighter airflow mimics cigarettes; open airflow produces more vapor.

## Device categories

| Type | Description | Target user |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Disposable | Pre-filled, non-rechargeable, single-use | Convenience, low commitment |
| Pod system | Small, rechargeable, uses pre-filled or refillable pods | Simplicity, portability |
| AIO (all-in-one) | Integrated tank and battery, refillable | Moderate customization |
| Box mod + tank | Separate battery mod and tank, adjustable wattage | Full control, hobbyist |
| Mechanical mod | No electronics, direct battery-to-coil connection | Advanced users only |

<Alert tone="warning" title="Mechanical mod risk">
Mechanical mods have no short-circuit protection, no voltage regulation, and no safety cutoffs. Misuse (wrong coil resistance, damaged battery wraps, hybrid connections with incompatible atomizers) can cause battery venting or explosion. They are not appropriate for beginners.
</Alert>

## E-liquid composition

Most e-liquids contain four base ingredients:

- **Propylene glycol (PG)** -- thin liquid that carries flavor well and produces a "throat hit" similar to cigarettes.
- **Vegetable glycerin (VG)** -- thicker liquid that produces denser vapor clouds. Higher VG ratios are smoother but can clog smaller coils.
- **Nicotine** -- available in freebase form (traditional) or nicotine salt form (smoother at higher concentrations, common in pods).
- **Flavorings** -- food-grade flavorings adapted for inhalation. Thousands of flavor profiles exist.

### Nicotine concentrations

| Format | Typical range | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Freebase nicotine | 0-12 mg/mL | Used in higher-power devices, harsher above ~6 mg/mL |
| Nicotine salt | 20-50 mg/mL | Smooth at high concentrations, designed for low-power pods |

<Alert tone="info" title="Nicotine salt vs freebase">
Nicotine salts use benzoic acid to lower the pH, making high nicotine concentrations smoother to inhale. This innovation is what made pod-style devices effective as cigarette alternatives -- and also what makes them highly addictive for non-smokers.
</Alert>

## Health considerations

Vaping is not risk-free. Key health concerns:

- **Nicotine addiction** -- nicotine is highly addictive regardless of delivery method. Pod devices with salt nicotine can deliver cigarette-level nicotine very efficiently.
- **Respiratory effects** -- inhaling heated aerosol exposes lungs to chemicals not present in clean air. Long-term effects are still being studied.
- **EVALI** -- in 2019, a lung injury outbreak was linked primarily to vitamin E acetate in illicit THC cartridges, not commercial nicotine products. This highlighted the danger of unregulated additives.
- **Youth uptake** -- flavored products and discreet devices drove rapid adoption among minors, creating a new generation of nicotine-dependent users.

### Relative risk framing

Public health agencies generally agree that vaping is less harmful than combustible cigarettes for adult smokers who switch completely. However, "less harmful than cigarettes" is a low bar. For non-smokers, there is no health benefit to starting.

## Regulation

Vaping regulation varies widely:

- **US** -- FDA regulates e-cigarettes as tobacco products. Pre-market authorization is required. Flavored cartridge sales have been restricted.
- **UK** -- relatively permissive. Vaping is actively promoted as a smoking cessation tool by public health bodies.
- **EU** -- Tobacco Products Directive limits nicotine concentration to 20 mg/mL, tank size to 2 mL, and requires health warnings.
- **Australia** -- nicotine e-liquid requires a prescription.
- **Some countries** (India, Brazil, Thailand) have banned vaping products entirely.

<Alert tone="warning" title="Health note">
This page is educational only. Vaping carries health and dependency risks, especially with nicotine use. Follow applicable law and rely on qualified medical guidance for health questions.
</Alert>

## Practical study approach

1. Understand the basic mechanism: battery, coil, wick, liquid, aerosol.
2. Learn the difference between device categories and why they exist (convenience vs control tradeoff).
3. Study e-liquid composition and why PG/VG ratio and nicotine format matter.
4. Read the public health literature on relative risk -- understand what is established vs what is still unknown.
5. Follow regulatory developments in your jurisdiction, as the legal landscape changes frequently.
