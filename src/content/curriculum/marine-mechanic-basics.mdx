## What makes marine mechanics unique

Marine engines face corrosion, moisture exposure, and seasonal use patterns. Maintenance quality has a direct impact on on-water safety.

## Key systems to master

| System | Focus areas |
| --- | --- |
| Fuel | Water contamination checks, filter service |
| Cooling | Raw-water flow, impeller condition, overheating causes |
| Electrical | Marine-grade wiring, grounding, battery isolation |
| Drive/propulsion | Prop condition, shaft alignment, lower unit checks |
| Corrosion control | Anodes, galvanic protection, rinse/flush routines |

## Practical seasonal workflow

1. Pre-season inspection and fluid service.
2. Start-up checks and leak verification.
3. In-season preventive maintenance.
4. Winterization and fuel stabilization.

<Alert tone="warning" title="Safety reminder">
Always ventilate enclosed engine spaces and confirm there are no fuel vapors before starting.
</Alert>

## Career entry ideas

- Begin in a marina service shop.
- Learn both outboard and inboard systems.
- Get comfortable reading manufacturer service literature.
- Build skills in corrosion prevention and electrical troubleshooting.
