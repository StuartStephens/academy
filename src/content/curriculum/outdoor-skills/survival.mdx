## What survival means

Survival is about staying alive through disciplined priorities when conditions become dangerous. In most environments, panic and poor decisions create more risk than lack of gear alone.

## Core survival priorities

| Priority | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Shelter/exposure | Heat loss or heat stress can become critical quickly |
| Water | Dehydration rapidly reduces judgment and performance |
| Signaling | Rescue is often safer than prolonged self-recovery |
| Medical issues | Small injuries can become major field problems |
| Navigation decisions | Movement without a plan can worsen risk |

## Survival approach

1. Stop and assess the situation.
2. Stabilize exposure and immediate safety.
3. Inventory water, tools, and communication options.
4. Signal clearly if rescue is appropriate.
5. Make conservative movement decisions.

<Alert tone="warning" title="Reality check">
Entertainment survival content often overemphasizes dramatic improvisation. In real emergencies, shelter, signaling, and calm decision-making usually matter more.
</Alert>

## Common mistakes

- Moving too fast without orientation.
- Ignoring changing weather.
- Delaying shelter setup.
- Treating untreated water as safe.
