## What bushcraft is

Bushcraft focuses on practical outdoor self-reliance through skill, observation, and careful use of tools and natural materials. It overlaps with camping and survival, but it emphasizes fieldcraft and sustainable problem solving more than emergency response.

## Core bushcraft topics

| Topic | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Firecraft | Fire supports warmth, cooking, signaling, and morale |
| Knife and tool use | Safe tool control enables many outdoor tasks |
| Shelter craft | Improvised shelter supports weather protection |
| Natural materials | Wood, cordage, and plant knowledge improve versatility |
| Observation | Tracking weather, terrain, and resources improves decisions |

## Skill progression

1. Learn safe knife and saw handling.
2. Practice fire starting under controlled conditions.
3. Build simple shelter systems before attempting complex ones.
4. Improve field observation and campcraft habits.
5. Focus on repeatable competence, not novelty.

## Common mistakes

- Treating bushcraft as performance instead of practical skill.
- Using tools without training or safety discipline.
- Damaging environments through careless harvesting.
- Practicing advanced skills before mastering basics.
