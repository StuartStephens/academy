## What camping requires

Camping is the planned practice of living outdoors temporarily with shelter, supplies, and a site routine. Good camping reduces avoidable risk through organization and preparation.

## Core camping topics

| Topic | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Site selection | Affects drainage, wind exposure, safety, and comfort |
| Shelter setup | Controls weather exposure and sleep quality |
| Sleep system | Temperature management is critical for recovery |
| Food and water | Safe storage and planning prevent avoidable problems |
| Camp routine | Clean systems reduce stress and forgotten tasks |

## Basic camping workflow

1. Check weather, terrain, and local rules.
2. Pack shelter, insulation, water, food, and lighting first.
3. Set camp before dark when possible.
4. Keep gear dry and food secured.
5. Break camp cleanly and leave the site in good condition.

## Common mistakes

- Bringing gear without testing it first.
- Underestimating temperature drop at night.
- Choosing a poor site with bad drainage or exposure.
- Not planning water access or treatment.
